In May and June of 2014, the Mayday Political Action Committee launched a bold experiment. We appealed to the citizens directly to raise the money we need to get a Congress committed to fundimental reform.

Now comes the tough part - fighting to elect representatives who will fix the broken system of corruption in Washington.

Here are the details of our plan.

About the MayDay PAC

We are a Super PAC to end all Super PACs*

* yes, including ourselves.

We are using the power of citizen-funded big money to fight the big money of crony capitalists and special interests on both the left and the right.

Ironic? Yes. Embrace the irony.

Our Mission

A Congress Dedicated to Fundimental Reform by 2016.

Where We've Been
We launched two crowdfunded campaigns. Both were successful in raising awareness of corruption, explaining the alternatives to corruption, and gathering the resources to fight against corruption.

We met our first $1M goal in only 13 days. That $1M was matched by technology entrepeneurs from all sides of the political debate.

In June, we set a higher bar - a $5M goal. We did not meet that goal because we did not allow ourselves enough time. Even so, in July, we got more than two and a half times as much in pledges than we did in May, and still raised $X.X million.
Where We're Going
Now the hard part: We're picking five key Congressional districts. We will independently support amazing candidates in their efforts to remove opponents entrenched in corruption.

We will not only win those races, but do it in a way that it is clear to all observers that the issue of corruption turned the tide of the election. But this is just the "pilot program."

In 2016, we will use what we've learned from those five races and start a much bigger initiative - one designed to get enough votes in Congress to pass fundimental reforms that change the way candidates fund elections.

Fixing democracy

Wealthy funders are holding our democracy hostage. But we'll get it back.

We need a team of legislators in Congress who will champion the public policies necessary to fix our broken government, starting by ending the stranglehold of big money on our political system.

The first thing we need to do is eliminate the conflict of interest between Congress's duty to serve the people and incentive to serve the funders by making the citizens the funders — Citizen-Funded Campaigns.

Through citizen-funded campaigns we can get Congress working for us again, without restricting anyone's right to free speech. (Or, for that matter, expensive speech.) We would not need to replace the sitting Justices of the Supreme Court or amend the Constitution… at least not to get the basic machinery of government working again.
