In May and June of 2014, the Mayday Political Action Committee launched a bold experiment. We appealed to the citizens directly to raise the money we need to get a Congress committed to fundamental reform.

That experiment was a wild success.

Now comes the tough part - fighting to elect representatives who will fix the broken system of corruption in Washington.

About the MayDay PAC

We are a crowdfunded Super PAC to end all Super PACs*

* yes, including ourselves.

We are using the power of citizen-funded big money to fight the big money of crony capitalists and special interests on both the left and the right.

Ironic? Yes. Embrace the irony.

Our Mission

A Congress Dedicated to Fundimental Reform by 2016.

Where We've Been
We launched two crowdfunded campaigns. We met our first $1M goal in only 13 days. That $1M was matched by technology entrepeneurs from all sides of the political debate.

In June, we set an even larger $5M goal, which over 30,000 people stepped forward to meet.
Where We're Going
Now the hard part: We're picking key Congressional districts and will independently support reform-minded candidates in their efforts to remove opponents who defend the ways of DC.

We will not only win those races, but do it in a way that it is clear to all observers that the issue of corruption turned the tide of the election.

Stay tuned to our blog for upcoming announcements.

Fixing democracy

The funders of campaigns are holding our democracy hostage.

We need a Congress that will fundamentally change the way elections are funded, and through that change, reduce the stranglehold of big money in our politics.

The reform we support will eliminate the conflict of interest between Congress's duty to serve the people and incentive to serve the funders by making the citizens the funders — Citizen-Funded Campaigns.

Through citizen-funded campaigns we can get Congress working for us again, without restricting anyone's right to free speech. (Or, for that matter, expensive speech.) We would not need to replace the sitting Justices of the Supreme Court or amend the Constitution… at least not to get the basic machinery of government working again.
